Recordings

Discover a comprehensive collection of all the music recorded by Hubert. The recordings span a wide range of genres, from haunting soundtrack music to pulsing electronic beats and folk songs. 

Instrumentals

Hubert's love for instrumental music has been a driving force in his productions, finding in it a way to express emotions that words cannot capture.

Inspirium
Inspirium is a piece that takes its roots in the format of the late 70s instrumental albums. It is all hand-played on various acoustic instruments, and features some fantastic guest musicians.
Once Upon a Rose
This music was written for an Irish dance show which premiered in Mexico City. Expect a lot of fiddle (and the occasional fairy).
Journeyman
This is a collection of pieces recorded over the years, featuring music for art exhibitions, musicals, videos, and various live shows.

Esthesia

"Esthesia" is Hubert's electrifying electronic music project. With its pulsing beats, soaring melodies, and otherworldly soundscapes, "Esthesia" transports listeners on a journey of the senses, fusing the latest technology with Hubert's unique musical vision to create something truly unique.

Technicolor
As close as we will get to a techno album! Most tracks come from experimenting with a modular synthesizer, and building something out of the result.
Analog
This was recorded using all analog equipment, from the synths to the various processors used to shape the sounds.
Colour and Pain
This album dates back to 1999, inspired by the Parisian goth scene. A rather intense recording from a rather intense period!
Little Tales
This album was almost entirely written on a laptop in various coffee shops in and around Toronto.
Sense
This album features all the production techniques Hubert had gathered by then - lots of sampling, sound design, and processing. It also happens to feature his bathtub.

Pop / Folk / Rock

Polymer
This is a 20 minute long uninterrupted progressive rock instrumental recording, inspired partly by the aquisition of a lovely 1978 vintage electric guitar.
Through the Veil
This album features Hubert on vocal duties, with a series of songs described as his most personal yet.

Collaborations

There is nothing like working with talented and like-minded musicians. This is when music comes alive and Hubert has found himself very lucky to have been part of several great collaborations.

The Thundermoons: Where it goes
The Thundermoon is a pop/rock band from Toronto, Canada. Hubert produced this EP, played bass, and co-wrote one of the songs.
Rare And Reworked - The Music Of Mike Oldfield
Hubert was invited to submit a track to celebrate the 50th anniversary of Tubular Bells. The idea was to choose and reinterpret a lesser known track. "I always thought that Music of the Spheres would make an interesting non-orchestral record, so this was the perfect opportunity to try it out"
Tubular World: Tubular Bells
Hubert was invited to participate in this cover of Mike Oldfield's groundbreaking album Tubular Bells. He performed the part called "Latin", in the first half of the record. Check out the official video.
